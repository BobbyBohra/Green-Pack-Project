Document: CPCB EPR Guidelines 2022 - Section 1
Source: Central Pollution Control Board, India

Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) Overview:
EPR is a policy approach under which producers are given significant responsibility for the treatment or disposal of post-consumer products.

Key Rules:
- Every plastic packaging producer must register with CPCB.
- Monthly declarations of plastic put into market must be submitted by the 10th of every following month.
- Categories covered: rigid plastic, flexible plastic, multilayer plastic.
- Producers must maintain records for at least 3 years.
- Annual EPR targets must be met failing which penalties apply.

Penalties:
- Non-submission of monthly declaration: Rs. 10,000 per month.
- False declaration: Rs. 50,000 and cancellation of registration.
- Non-achievement of EPR targets: Rs. 50 per kg of shortfall.
